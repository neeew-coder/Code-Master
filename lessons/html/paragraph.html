A paragraph always starts on a new line and is typically a block of text. Browsers automatically add spacing before and after paragraphs.

üìè Horizontal Rules

The <hr> tag defines a thematic break and is often rendered as a horizontal line. 

<!DOCTYPE html>
<html>
  <body>
    <h1>This is heading 1</h1>
    <p>This is some text.</p>
    <hr>
    <h2>This is heading 2</h2>
    <p>This is some other text.</p>
    <hr>
  </body>
</html>
  
<h1>This is heading 1</h1>
<p>This is some text.</p>

üìú The Poem Problem

HTML collapses whitespace, so poems written with line breaks will appear as a single line. 

<!DOCTYPE html>
<html>
  <body>
    <p>My Bonnie lies over the ocean. My Bonnie lies over the sea. My Bonnie lies over the ocean. Oh, bring back my Bonnie to me.</p>
  </body>
</html>
  
<p>
  My Bonnie lies over the ocean.

  My Bonnie lies over the sea.

  My Bonnie lies over the ocean.

  Oh, bring back my Bonnie to me.
</p>

üßæ Solution: The <pre> Element

Use <pre> to preserve whitespace and line breaks. It displays text in a fixed-width font.

<html>
  <body>
    <pre>
  My Bonnie lies over the ocean.

  My Bonnie lies over the sea.

  My Bonnie lies over the ocean.

  Oh, bring back my Bonnie to me.
    </pre>
  </body>
</html>

  My Bonnie lies over the sea.
