HTML Quotation and Citation Elements

HTML provides semantic elements for quotations, citations, abbreviations, contact info, and text direction control.

🧾 <blockquote> — Long Quotations

Defines a section quoted from another source. Browsers usually indent it. 

<p>Here is a quote from WWF's website:</p>
<blockquote cite="http://www.worldwildlife.org/who/index.html">
For 60 years, WWF has worked to help people and nature thrive...
</blockquote>

Here is a quote from WWF's website:

For 60 years, WWF has worked to help people and nature thrive...

💬 <q> — Short Quotations

Defines a short inline quotation. Browsers automatically add quotation marks. 

<p>WWF's goal is to: <q>Build a future where people live in harmony with nature.</q></p>

WWF's goal is to: Build a future where people live in harmony with nature.

🔤 <abbr> — Abbreviations

Defines an abbreviation or acronym. Use the title attribute to show full meaning on hover. 

<p>The <abbr title="World Health Organization">WHO</abbr> was founded in 1948.</p>

The WHO was founded in 1948.

📬 <address> — Contact Info

Defines contact information. Usually renders in italic with line breaks. 

<address>
Written by John Doe.<br>
Visit us at:<br>
Example.com<br>
Box 564, Disneyland<br>
USA
</address>
Written by John Doe.
Visit us at:
Example.com
Box 564, Disneyland
USA

🎨 <cite> — Creative Work Titles

Defines the title of a creative work. Usually renders in italic. 

<p><cite>The Scream</cite> by Edvard Munch. Painted in 1893.</p>

The Scream by Edvard Munch. Painted in 1893.

↔️ <bdo> — Bi-Directional Override

Overrides the current text direction. Use dir="rtl" for right-to-left. 

<bdo dir="rtl">This text will be written from right to left</bdo>
This text will be written from right to left
