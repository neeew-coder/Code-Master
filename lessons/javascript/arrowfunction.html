üß† Lesson Summary

Arrow functions in JavaScript offer a shorter syntax for writing functions and automatically inherit this from their surrounding scope. Unlike regular functions, they don‚Äôt have their own this, arguments, or super. This makes them ideal for callbacks and methods where lexical scoping is preferred. However, they should not be used as constructors or in places where dynamic this binding is required.
